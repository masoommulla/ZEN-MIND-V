**USE CASE DIAGRAM DESCRIPTION:**

The ZEN-MIND use case diagram illustrates a comprehensive mental wellness platform designed specifically for teenagers aged 13-19. The system serves three distinct actors with different capabilities and access levels.

**Unregistered Users** represent visitors who have not yet created an account. They can view the landing page to learn about the platform's features, register for a new account by providing their name, email, password, and age (validated to ensure they are between 13-19 years old), and login to access their account. The registration process automatically includes login functionality, creating a seamless onboarding experience.

**Users (Teens)** represent the primary audience - registered teenagers seeking mental wellness support. Once authenticated, they gain access to a rich ecosystem of features organized into five main categories:

1. *Profile Management*: Users can update their personal information (name, email, phone, gender), change their password securely, and logout when finished.

2. *Mental Wellness Tracking*: The core functionality includes mood tracking where users log their daily emotional state with an intensity scale (1-10), select specific emotions, and add contextual notes. The system automatically generates AI-powered suggestions for activities, books, or songs based on mood entries. Users can view their mood history with statistical insights and delete entries as needed. The journaling feature allows users to create detailed entries with titles, content, mood associations, and tags. Journals can be edited, marked as favorites, searched by content, deleted, and viewed with comprehensive statistics showing total entries and popular tags.

3. *Productivity Tools*: Users manage their tasks through a todo system that supports categorization (personal, study, health, other), priority levels, completion toggling, editing, and deletion. Task statistics show completion rates and progress. Study plans help users organize their academic work by creating sessions with subject, topic, duration, and notes. Study sessions can be marked complete, edited, deleted, and tracked with total study time statistics. A calendar view integrates journals, todos, and study plans for holistic life management.

4. *AI and Resources*: Users can chat with an AI companion powered by Botpress Cloud for 24/7 mental health support. The resources section provides curated motivational videos (via YouTube embeds), calming music playlists (via Spotify/YouTube links), and mini-games including Tic-Tac-Toe, Memory Match, and Breathing Exercises for stress relief and entertainment.

5. *Professional Therapy Services*: Users can browse a list of licensed therapists, search by name or specialization, and sort by rating, experience, or cost. Each therapist profile displays comprehensive information including their about section, education credentials, years of experience, pricing per session, languages spoken, specializations, and user reviews with ratings. Real-time status indicators show if therapists are currently busy in a session or available. The booking process follows an instant booking model (not slot-based scheduling). Users select their desired session duration (30/45/60 minutes, though all sessions default to 30 minutes currently), complete a fake payment via UPI or Credit Card simulation, and receive immediate appointment confirmation. The system generates a Jitsi video conference link automatically. Users must wait 5 minutes from the booking time before they can join the session - this wait period is strictly enforced by the system. During the wait, users see a countdown timer. After the wait period, users can join the video session via Jitsi, which includes a real-time countdown showing remaining session time. Users can view all their appointments (upcoming and past), cancel scheduled sessions (which releases the therapist's time slot), view their session history, and submit reviews with ratings and comments after completed sessions.

**Therapists** are licensed mental health professionals who access the platform through a separate therapist portal. They have three main areas of functionality:

1. *Authentication and Profile*: Therapists login using their credentials. They can update their professional profile including name, email, profile photo, about section, education credentials, years of experience, languages spoken, and specializations. Profile pictures can be uploaded as files or specified via URL. Password management includes the ability to change password with current password verification, or reset password via OTP if forgotten. The OTP reset flow involves requesting an OTP sent to their email, verifying the 6-digit code, and setting a new password. Therapists can logout to end their session securely.

2. *Session Management*: Therapists view their appointments filtered to show only today's sessions, sorted chronologically. Each appointment displays the session time, duration, and a live countdown timer. The countdown shows when the session becomes available to join (5 minutes after booking), when it starts, and when it ends. Therapists can only join sessions after 5 minutes have elapsed from the booking time - this prevents sessions from starting before both parties are ready. When joining, therapists enter a Jitsi video conference with the user. During the session, a real-time countdown monitors the remaining time. The system automatically ends sessions after the duration expires, though therapists can also manually end sessions. Session status updates automatically from scheduled to completed.

3. *Settings*: Therapists can update their pricing by setting a price per session anywhere from â‚¹100 to â‚¹5000 (recommended range â‚¹400-1000). The session duration is fixed at 30 minutes. Therapists can view their current active pricing at any time.

**System Automation** represents nine automated processes that run without user intervention:

1. *Generate Meeting Link*: Automatically creates a unique Jitsi conference room link when a user books an appointment, ensuring privacy and uniqueness for each session.

2. *Enforce 5-Minute Wait*: Strictly prevents both users and therapists from joining sessions before 5 minutes have elapsed from booking time, ensuring both parties have time to prepare.

3. *Update Session Status*: Automatically transitions appointment status from scheduled to completed after sessions end, maintaining accurate records.

4. *Calculate Statistics*: Continuously computes metrics for mood tracking (average mood, trends), journal entries (total count, popular tags), todos (completion rates), and study plans (total study hours), providing users with insights into their progress.

5. *Generate AI Suggestions*: Triggers after mood logging to analyze the user's emotional state and provide personalized recommendations for activities (meditation, exercise, journaling), books (self-help, fiction), and songs (calming, uplifting), helping users take positive action.

6. *Verify Payment*: Processes fake payment transactions (simulated UPI/Credit Card) and creates confirmed appointments only after successful payment, teaching users about the booking flow.

7. *Auto-End Session*: Monitors active video sessions and automatically marks them complete when the duration (30 minutes) expires, ensuring sessions don't run indefinitely.

8. *Update Therapist Busy Status*: Dynamically sets therapist availability status to "busy" when they join a video session and "available" when the session ends, helping users see real-time availability.

9. *Send OTP Email*: Generates and emails a 6-digit OTP code to therapists requesting password reset, with expiration for security.

**External Systems Integration:**

The platform integrates with six external services:

1. *Botpress Cloud*: Powers the AI chatbot for conversational mental health support, accessible 24/7 for users needing immediate assistance.

2. *Jitsi Video*: Provides the video conferencing infrastructure for therapy sessions, chosen for its open-source nature and ease of integration.

3. *YouTube*: Hosts and streams motivational videos embedded in the resources section, offering visual mental wellness content.

4. *Spotify*: Provides curated playlists of calming music linked from the resources section for mood enhancement and stress relief.

5. *MongoDB Atlas*: Serves as the cloud database backend storing all user data, therapist profiles, appointments, mood entries, journals, todos, study plans, and reviews with proper data isolation between users.

6. *Email Service*: Handles OTP delivery for therapist password reset functionality, ensuring secure password recovery.

**Key Design Decisions:**

1. *Instant Booking vs Slot-Based*: The platform uses instant booking rather than traditional slot-based scheduling. Users book sessions immediately with available therapists rather than selecting from pre-defined time slots. This simplifies the user experience but means therapists cannot manually control their availability schedule.

2. *5-Minute Wait Period*: Both users and therapists must wait 5 minutes after booking before joining sessions. This wait period serves multiple purposes: gives both parties time to prepare mentally, prevents accidental early joining, and simulates a realistic waiting room experience. The countdown timer creates anticipation and ensures readiness.

3. *Fixed 30-Minute Sessions*: Although a duration selector exists in the UI (30/45/60 minutes), all sessions are currently implemented as 30 minutes. This standardization simplifies scheduling and ensures consistent therapist availability.

4. *Fake Payment System*: The platform uses simulated payment processing (fake UPI and Credit Card) rather than real payment integration. This demonstrates the booking flow without requiring payment gateway setup, making it ideal for demonstration and MVP testing.

5. *No Slot Management UI*: While the backend APIs support therapists adding and removing availability slots, the frontend does not provide UI for this functionality. This was a deliberate scope decision - the platform currently relies on instant booking availability rather than slot-based scheduling. Future versions could add slot management UI if slot-based scheduling is desired.

6. *Age Restriction*: The platform specifically serves teenagers aged 13-19 years, enforced during registration. This demographic focus allows tailored content, design aesthetics (cartoon-modern with anime graphics), and language appropriate for teens.

7. *Data Isolation*: Strong data isolation ensures users can only access their own mood entries, journals, todos, and study plans. Therapists only see their own appointments and profile. This privacy-first approach builds trust with the sensitive nature of mental health data.
